Evolving ITSM knowledge findability and reducing human interaction costs using Generative AI

Helping a British oil giant implement generative AI to transform its internal IT support experience through agentic workflows and smarter knowledge discovery.

Snapshot
The "too long, didn't read".

Company

bp plc

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

24 weeks (Discovery → MVP handover)

Scale

Enterprise (~87,000 users)

Team

1 Product Owner, 1 Programme Manager, 2 Experience Designers, 4 ServiceNow Developers

Impact

  • 28% reduction in repeat tickets
  • CSAT increase from 3.6 → 4.4
  • Average resolution time by 22%

    • Tools

    ServiceNow, Figma, FigJam, Sketch, Zeplin, Mural, Azure DevOps

    The business
    Context, constraints, why it mattered.

    The organisation's IT service desk is overwhelmed with repetitive and simple queries, leading to high ticket volume and interaction costs. Employees struggle to find self-serve answers, increasing traffic to live support and reducing productivity.

    As part of a wider product strategy – Generative AI was explored as a lever to encourage self-serve behaviour through surfacing answers, summarising knowledge and thus improving operational efficiency.

    ~40%
    of service desk tickets are repeatable, low-complexity queries (Level 1 and below). Think password resets, or “how-to” questions.

    8x
    Live agent support interactions cost more than successful self-serve resolutions.

    42%
    of employees fail to resolve their issue through existing self-serve channels.

    My role
    Contributions and collaborations.

    As the lead designer in the squad, I led the end-to-end design process –– from research, workshops, journey mapping, interaction design, to prototyping and usability testing.

    I oversaw the integration of the organisation's design system into the IT support experience - requiring close collaboration between myself, our ServiceNow developers and Design Engineering.

    By embracing the product triad framework, I ensured to align our collaborative efforts between product, design and development through regular check-ins and bi-weekly sprint cycles to keep us agile.

    Goals & KPIs
    Defining what we want to solve.

    Functional and non-functional goals were categorised to balance user needs, business objectives, and technical constraints.

    I implemented MoSCoW priorisation to create a feature backlog that aligns with our product goals. As for high-level goals, we established the following three:

    Goal #1

    Reduce live support interactions for common queries by 20–30% (MVP target).

    Goal #2

    Increase successful self-serve resolution / search success rate to >75–85% for top queries.

    Goal #3

    Preserve a concise interaction flow (3 steps) and maintain WCAG 2.1 compliance.

    Discovery and research
    What user insights did we gather?

    This was an immense task that required diligence and understanding of the root problem.

    Reviewing the data

    I began with working with our data team to dive deep into the ServiceNow analytics:

    • 41% of tickets were "how‑to" questions already answered in knowledge articles
    • Qualitative data indicated that employees didn’t trust search results due to outdated content.
    • Knowledge base articles were averaging a length of ~840 words, causing drop-off and straight to human support.

    1-to-1 interviews with SMEs and power users

    12 stakeholder interviews were carried out across three regions (US, Europe, APAC) to build out personas and further understand pain points in both angles. These insights, combined with analytics, were later presented to leadership for clarity and direction.

    Discovery workshop with executives and SMEs

    I delivered a workshop with executives and subject matter experts to outline business goals, brainstorm ideas, and present the user insights gathered. It was understood that leadership were in favour for GenAI integration as a cost-saving solution and shift over-reliance on the IT service desk.

    Problem definition
    Turning research into a clear design focus.

    Essentially, there were three sides to the problem that required a major overhaul to the ITSM experience flow, for employees and the team.

    “Employees need a fast, trustworthy way to get answers or understand ticket progress without feeling like they’re navigating an IT system.”

    Meanwhile...

    "Service desk agents were decreasing in productivity due to excessive level 1 tickets."

    And for the business...

    "Operational costs and efficiency were at risk due to over-reliance on face-to-face, human IT support."
